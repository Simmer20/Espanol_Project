Famous Spanish Festivals

  • San Jordi's Day: A romantic celebration in Catalonia, similar to Valentine's Day.
  • Patios de Córdoba: Flower-filled courtyards open to the public each May.
  • La Tomatina: The world-famous tomato fight in Buñol.
  • Three Kings Day: Major gift-giving celebration on January 6.
  • Cinco de Mayo: Celebrated more in Mexico and the U.S., but notable in Spanish-speaking cultures.
  • Caimada: A Galician fire ritual involving a spell and flaming punch.

Language & Dialects

Spanish (Castilian) is the official language, but Spain is home to many regional dialects:

  • Catalan (Catalonia)
  • Basque (Euskadi)
  • Galician (Galicia)
  • Valencian (Valencia)
  • Andalusian (South)

Religion in Spain

Spain has a deep-rooted Catholic heritage visible in its traditions, festivals, and architecture. Semana Santa (Holy Week) features processions across the country.

Museums & Architecture

  • Architecture: From Roman ruins and Moorish palaces to Gaudí's surreal creations like the Sagrada Família.
  • Museums: The Prado Museum in Madrid, Guggenheim in Bilbao, and more hold masterpieces by Velázquez, Picasso, Dalí, and Goya.
